A Market and Channel Strategy Definition is established, from a Market and Channel analysis, in respect with the strategic business direction.<br/>It formalizes, in a Market Strategy Plan, the Market and Channel key stakes, strategic axes and multi years objectives for the company, as well as Market and Channel objectives, segmentation and targeting, positioning.<br/>It includes: <br/>• forecasts of the Market and Channel sales. Forecasts are carried out from a chronological modeling of the tendencies, the forecasts of commercial actions or marketing.<br/>• identification of market segment, customer profile and channels used across the enterprise<br/>• definition of behaviors models and strategy:<br/>     • development of purchase behavior models that will be used for the marketing strategy development, in order to improve the distribution performance. These models will be used for the statistical tool implementation (customer profile type) and the creation of statistical data (dashboards).<br/>     • a broad Sales Strategy to support the market strategy with a Sales model management defining the various stages of the sales pipeline, which can be based on the SPANCO method (Suspect qualification, Prospect qualification, Analyzing, Negotiating, Closing, Ordering).<br/>
