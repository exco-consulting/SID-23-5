<<TMF_AF>>
The Marketing Campaign Design Aggregate Function concerns the phase of the preparation of a defined campaign in a multichannel environment to deliver one-to-one or “right-time” campaigns. It establishes the targeted customers list and/or criteria, the proposition, visuals, waves, planning and metrics to evaluate the campaign. It extracts the target list to prepare the execution on channels and provides the campaign brief.<br/><br/>• the targeted customers list definition is based both on the criteria held in the Campaign Definition function and on the analysis of the existing data (e.g. analytics) or of predictive modeling for a specific campaign. The targeting takes into account the management of the commercial pressure from the event history of the proactive and reactive campaigns and the events of contacts concerning the targeted customers. <br/>• new type of campaigns uses the geofencing infrastructure to push messages, promotions such as coupons, advertising, to customers when they enter or leave a designated area and are localized by beacons. These campaigns need to define content push rules and personal context, and to check customer opt-in.<br/>• the proposition that will be addressed to each customer sub-segment is described in terms of generosity, requested effort and validity period, and event triggers.<br/>• the visuals supporting the message (mailing & e-mailing channels) are created <br/>• the campaign is split in waves, each of them is associated with a sales channel and a communication mode.<br/>• the campaign’s business case, metrics and KPIS are defined as well. <br/>• the campaign message definition<br/>• and eventually policy rules definition according to event-based marketing capabilities to trigger automated campaigns.<br/><br/>The main outputs of this function are the campaign brief, mix and metrics.<br/>
