Sourcing Savings Management Aggregate Function is part of Purchasing process and aims to get cost reduction and/or cost avoidance.<br/>It consists in comparing estimated budget with approved purchase order payments. <br/><br/>Cost reductions, understood as tangible bottom line reductions, are easily defined as / characterized by: <br/>•  year-on-year saving over the constant volume of purchased product/service,<br/>•  direct reduction of expense liked to changes in processes/technology/policies that directly reduces expenses, <br/>•  process improvements that result in real and measurable cost or asset reductions, <br/>•  examination of existing products or services, contractual agreements, or processes to determine potential changes that reduce cost, and <br/>•  net reductions in prices paid for items procured when compared to prices in place for the prior 12 months or a change to lower cost alternatives. <br/><br/>Cost avoidance is much more difficult to define. Suggested definitions include: <br/>•  avoidance is a cost reduction that does not lower the cost of products/ but rather minimizes or avoids entirely the negative impact to the bottom line that a price increase would have caused, <br/>•  when there is an increase in output/capacity without increasing resource expenditure, in general, the cost avoidance savings are the amount that would have been spent to handle the increased volume/output, <br/>• avoidances include process improvements that do not immediately reduce cost or assets but provide benefits through improved process efficiency, employee productivity, improved customer satisfaction, improved competitiveness, etc.; over time, cost avoidance often becomes cost savings.<br/>
