The Security Strategy Definition and Management function allows to better anticipate, understand and prevent risks, related to the security of people, assets and information by defining the CSP Security Policy.<br/><br/>It includes: <br/>• The identification of risk and risk level on various security aspects<br/>• The definition and implementation of security rules and the means to verify compliance (processes, tools, organisation, etc.).<br/>• The management of major crises (technical, environmental, etc.)<br/>• The management of Safety Audits<br/>• The security context monitoring<br/><br/>Security concerns several parts: <br/>• CSP financial and legal security<br/>• Personnel security (employees in their work environment, customers)<br/>• Site security (buildings)<br/>• Network, Service Platform and IS infrastructure security<br/>• Information security (GDPR, confidentiality, defence secrecy)<br/><br/>The CSP Security Policy may be applied to these different parts, and its implementation may be carried out in operational functions of collection, control, supervision and analysis.<br/>
