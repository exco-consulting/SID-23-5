Resource Trouble Resolution Aggregate Function is responsible for repair or replacement of faulty resources or using redundant equipment to replace the faulty resource after a trouble has been detected, in order to bring back the installed resource to a normal operational state as efficiently as possible.  <br/>This may involve automated or manual restoration activities. It may also involve reconfiguration, and/or dispatches of field technicians.<br/>These resolution actions can be temporary or palliative, to quickly stop the trouble, and followed by other actions which will definitely treat the trouble cause (ex: when a telegraph pole falls it can be first strengthened then replaced). So different resolution actions can be planned and executed, associated to the same Trouble. <br/>When we try palliative action and notice that it doesn’t solve the trouble, it’s not always necessary or interesting to undo it (ex: change of copper pair).<br/>These actions can be treated through resource order(s) (ex: reconfiguration of an equipment, installation of a new equipment) or project management (ex: scheduled work) when several tasks have to be planned and coordinated.<br/>Remark: If the resolution action is a Resource Order, this will be created via the Resource Order Initialization function.<br/>
