External Product Offering Development function includes the definition of, and sometimes the creation of, Product Offerings provided either by a Business Partner via the Service Provider, or provided with the Business Partner in conjunction with the Service Provider.  <br/><br/>This function differs from internal Product Offering Development in that this function addresses both the collaborative aspects of inter-company product Offering development and the divisions of responsibilities, costs and benefits among the partners.<br/><br/>This function provides the means to describe Product Offerings, according to marketing strategy:<br/> - commercial name<br/> - packaging rules of the contract: mandatory offers, optional offers, offers that can be ordered in number (ex: 1 to 4 mobile lines)<br/> - commercial incompatibilities or pre-requisites (ex: necessary to be the holder of a X contract to subscribe Y contract)<br/> - available commercial operations (ex: contract migration)<br/> - available commitment durations<br/> - any commercial criteria such as authorized sales channel or geographic area, customer criteria, etc.<br/> - tariff specifications and possible alterations. They are associated to the offer, to commercial operations or usage types and can be recurring or one shot. They are expressed as rules that can consider many criteria (ex: commitment duration, product configuration, sales channel, customer's age, etc.) and will be evaluated during the order capture process, or during the rating process for usage.<br/><br/>It includes facilities to design a new Product Offering based on an existing one and integrity rules controls.<br/>
