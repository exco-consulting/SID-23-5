Business Partner Revenue Sharing Model Development develops, maintains, and removes Business Partner revenue sharing models that define how revenue is shared by and enterprise with one or more other parties. Revenue sharing models are a key component revenue sharing agreements.<br/>This process reuses the Product Offering Pricing processes to manage the lifecycle of the amounts of revenue shared. For example, an enterprise may charge 10 euros for an offering and share a flat 4 euros with another Business Partner, such as an application developer. For configurable offerings the revenue shared may be dependent on those features chosen by the Business Partner who acquires the offering. In this case the revenue shared is also governed by the configuration of the Business Partner's product. A similar case applies to usage based revenue sharing.  Flat rate and usage based revenue sharing models are just two of a number of models, which also include but are not limited to tiered, commitment, progressive, along with multi-Business Partner variations of each. <br/><br/>Business Partner specific revenue sharing models may be prepared in advance and negotiated  as part of entering into an agreement with a Business Partner to be on-boarded and on-boarding their offerings.<br/>
