<<TMF_Process>>
Within the fraud practice, certain processes are formulated to provide consistency and control around analysis of suspected fraud and the supporting investigative work to determine whether a given case is, in fact, fraud.  Analysis and identification procedure policies may include M&P information covering:<br/><br/>- When an issue is eligible for investigation (e.g., meeting certain alarming criteria, having a high enough financial value, etc.). <br/>- How issues are prioritized once deemed eligible for investigation (ranking in work queue, assignment to rapid teams vs. longer term investigation teams, etc.)<br/>- What investigative processes are involved (e.g., internal process investigation, external agency investigation, internal network or system investigation, etc.)<br/>- Which individuals should conduct specific investigative steps (some steps may involve managerial levels for information access, etc.)<br/>- What supporting information may be necessary to conduct investigative analysis<br/>- What criteria should be met to deem an incident as actual fraud<br/>- What follow-up actions or steps are taken at the close of an incident (rules changes, policy changes, process changes, etc.)<br/><br/>The Analysis and Identification policies should not only contain this information, but also be able to provide this information as a guide to analysts, readily available and easily employed.<br/>
