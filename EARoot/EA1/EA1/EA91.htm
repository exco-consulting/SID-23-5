The design and visual language of MODA objects adhere to a set of specific guidelines, largely aligning with ArchiMate conventions, for ease of use and comprehension:<br/><br/><br/><ul><li><b>ArchiMate conventions</b>: The fundamental look and feel of MODA objects are based on ArchiMate language, providing a familiar visual context for those acquainted with ArchiMate.</li><li><b>Background color</b>: The background color of MODA objects signifies their association with a particular architectural domain. This use of color aids in the quick identification of the domain to which an object belongs.</li><li><b>Top right decorator</b>: In the top left corner of each object, a symbol known as a decorator mirrors its ArchiMate equivalent. This consistency helps many practitioners easily recognize the purpose or nature of the shape. If an entirely new concept is introduced, a new symbol is created to represent it.</li><li><b>TM Forum indicator</b>: A red dot positioned in the bottom left corner, accompanied by the letters 'TMF', signifies that the object has been augmented with additional information specific to the TM Forum. This enhancement provides context and value unique to the TM Forum environment.</li><li><b>Additional diagrams indicator</b>: On occasions, an object <a href="javascript:top.guidLink('{495563A6-3C00-4cb8-BDEE-03D02E43D230}')"><span style="color:#0000ff;"><u>may contain additional diagrams</u></span></a>. In such instances, a symbol resembling a horizontal figure-eight is placed in the right corner of the object, signaling the presence of more detailed visual information within the object.</li></ul>
<br/>These organized elements collectively enhance the usability and clarity of MODA, making it an efficient tool for representing and understanding complex digital architectures.<br/>
