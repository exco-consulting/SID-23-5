<font color="#29313b">In January 2010, the TM Forum received a generous contribution of intellectual property from the National Security Agency (NSA).  This contribution, a Net-Defense (NetD) data model, Implementation Guide, and accompanying data dictionary, has provided the TM Forum valuable insight on how to model information related to threats, incidents, events, assets, and vulnerabilities.  Several standards, such as the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Security Content Automation Protocol (SCAP) suite of standards, were embraced during the creation of the NetD model.</font><br/><font color="#29313b">This contribution combined with strong member commitments to this topic have expedited projects aimed at integrating Security Management into the TM Forum Frameworx.</font><br/><font color="#29313b"><br/></font><font color="#29313b">At the highest level, key information revolves around understanding:</font><br/><ul>
<li><font color="#29313b">What <u>assets</u> defenders are charged with protecting</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">What <u>vulnerabilities</u> the assets have</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">What <u>threats</u> are targeting the vulnerabilities on those assets</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">What <u>events</u> are occurring on the network</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">What <u>incidents</u> result from assessments of the events</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">What <u>systems</u> are, or may be impacted by successful exploits of vulnerabilities</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">Where assets, and their associated vulnerabilities, exist within the defense-in-depth infrastructure represented by surrounding <u>networks</u></font></li><li><font color="#29313b">What <u>policies</u> are in effect that direct how devices and networks should be configured, how vulnerabilities are remediated, and how incidents and events are to be recorded and reported?</font></li></ul>
<font color="#29313b"><i>Note: While the TM Forum has incorporated the NSA’s “Network Defense Data Model” into the Information Framework (SID), classes and attributes associated with “missions”, “operations”, “policies”, and “systems” were intentionally excluded.</i></font><br/><font color="#29313b">In order to align with the NIST SCAP Release 1.2, the TM Forum extended the Information Framework Enterprise Security ABE with Security Vulnerability Scoring for the Frameworx 12.5 release.  The NIST SCAP standard supports three types of scoring to calculate the severity of a particular vulnerability: the Common Configuration Scoring System (CCSS)<sup>1</sup>, the Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS)<sup>2</sup>, and the Common Misuse Scoring System (CMSS)<sup>3</sup>. While each of the scoring systems applies a different algorithm to calculate a score, the approach to scoring (use of metrics, standard values, and interim calculations) between the three systems is quite similar.  So, the TM Forum was able to model the Security Vulnerability Scoring classes, attributes and relationships in a generalized way that in the end supports all three scoring systems.  </font><br/>-------------------------<br/><font color="#29313b"><sup>1</sup> </font><a href="file://http://csrc.nist.gov/publications/nistir/ir7502/nistir-7502_CCSS.pdf"><font color="#0000ff"><u>http://csrc.nist.gov/publications/nistir/ir7502/nistir-7502_CCSS.pdf</u></font></a><font color="#29313b"> </font><br/><font color="#29313b"><sup>2</sup> </font><a href="file://http://csrc.nist.gov/publications/nistir/ir7435/NISTIR-7435.pdf"><font color="#0000ff"><u>http://csrc.nist.gov/publications/nistir/ir7435/NISTIR-7435.pdf</u></font></a><font color="#29313b"> </font><br/><font color="#29313b"><sup>3</sup> </font><a href="file://http://csrc.nist.gov/publications/drafts/nistir-7517/Draft-NISTIR-7517.pdf"><font color="#0000ff"><u>http://csrc.nist.gov/publications/drafts/nistir-7517/Draft-NISTIR-7517.pdf</u></font></a><font color="#29313b"> </font><br/>
