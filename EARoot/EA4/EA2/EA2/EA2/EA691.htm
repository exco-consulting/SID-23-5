Figure C.03a - Customer Product Order Item Business Entities diagram
One or many PartyRoles might be involved in a CustomerProductOrder / CustomerProductOrderItem such as Distributor, Holder, Buyer or Product User.<br/>Each CustomerProductOrderItem requires an action (AllowedProductAction) on a ProductSpecification or a ProductOffering.<br/>The configuration required is described by the related Product even if it isn’t delivered yet. The prices applicable are related to the Product (ProductPrice) or to ProductOrderItem / PorductOrder when it corresponds to prices not specific to a Product such as a global of 5% on ProductOrder one-time fees.<br/>To deliver the corresponding Product, the Place to deliver and Appointment(s) might be needed. An Appointment is an arrangement to do something or meet someone at a particular time and location.<br/>A CustomerProductOrderItem may also specify the commitment chosen by the Customer (i.e. CommitmentTermOrCondition).<br/>The CustomerProductOrderItems may specify the CustomerBillingAccount to which the Products’ are charged to.<br/>When CustomerProductOrderItems are not charged to a CustomerBillingAccount and carry one-time charges, they might be directly paid through one or many CustomerPayments. The same CustomerPayment may concern several ProductPrices.<br/>CustomerProductOrderItem may reference ServicOrderItems or ResourceOrderItems that participate in the Product required delivery.<br/><i>Relationships with ServicOrderItem, ResourceOrderItem, CustomerBillingAccount and CustomerPayment are not shared with Business Partner orders.</i><br/>
