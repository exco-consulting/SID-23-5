Figure C.12-01 – Issues with Using Composite/Atomic Charges to Model Financial Reporting diagram
<font color="#29313b">The ultimate goal of any billing process is creation of a bill (an invoice). The bill is created during a customer account billing cycle and associated with a customer account.</font><br/><font color="#29313b">For financial reasons it is critical that each AtomicAppliedCustomerBillingRate amount appears exactly once on each CustomerBill. This is not well supported by the atomic/Composite pattern as explained in the diagram.</font><br/><font color="#29313b">For financial reasons we must report $0.88, however summing all these instances together gives us $2.56. If we sum, however, only the leaves of the tree (the AtomicAppliedCustomerBillingRates) we get exactly $0.88 ($1 - $0.2 + $.05 + $.03). Therefore the FinancialCharge business entity aggregates only AtomicAppliedCustomerBillingRate</font><br/>
