The NetworkForwardingDomain (NFD) object class represents the capacity to enable forwarding and potential for forwarding via ForwardingReleationshipEncapsulation (FRE) constructs. The NFD represents the capability to define a route for forwarding across the contained network. <br/><br/><br/><br/>The NFD represents capability for one or more specific layerProtocols where the relevant boundaries for each layerProtocol coincide appropriately<br/><br/><br/><br/>It a grouping of zero or more:<br/><br/>- TPEs which represent access to the NFD<br/><br/>- subordinate NFDs which are a partition of its capacity (and hence completely contained)<br/><br/>- TLs between the grouped subordinate NFDs<br/><br/><br/><br/>The TPEs associated may terminate TopologicalLinks (TLs). NFD instances and TL instances together define the network topology.<br/><br/><br/><br/>New TPEs may be assigned to the NFD and TPEs may be unassigned from the NFD.<br/><br/><br/><br/>Note that the NFD object may be used to represent routing domains or other administrative domains. The domain groupings may be orthogonal.<br/><br/><br/><br/>The top level NFD corresponds to the whole network for the layerProtocols it includes.<br/>
