Figure R.32 - Content Charging Offer Price diagram
Shown in the following Figure is an example where the high definition (HD) format for the movie 2012 has one price, while the same movie has a different price for standard (STD) format.<br/>The STD and HD values do not have to be repeated as instances of CharacteristicSpecValues.  The Format instance of CharacteristicSpecification can be shared among all movie titles.  The names of the objects in the diagram are illustrative only.  If the price varied only by HD and STD and not by title then dependencies could be created that were only related to the format values.<br/>If the pay per view price was the same for every movie, then there would be no need for this type of pricing.  The current Product Offering to Product Offering Price association would be support this.  Also, the ProductOfferingPrice name is illustrative only; the price can be shared by multiple CharacteristicPriceDependencies.<br/>Note that an attribute may be required in Product Offering and/or Product Specification to indicate that choices can be made when accessing an associated Product.<br/>By relating price dependencies to the “use” entities:<br/><ul>
<li>A VoD subscription offering with unlimited free viewing with a fixed subscription price can be created by not associating use attributes with price dependencies and associating a ProductOfferingPrice to the VoD subscription offering</li><li>A VoD subscription offering with unlimited free viewing except for “special” movies can be created by associating only the specials uses with price dependencies and associating a ProductOfferingPrice to the VoD subscription offering</li><li>A VoD pay per view offering can be created by associating appropriate use attributes, such as title and format, with price dependencies.</li></ul><p/>
