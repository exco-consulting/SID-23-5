2. Equipment Entity Identification Format diagram
<font color="#29313b">A standardized code that uniquely identifies a type of manufactured telecommunications network equipment or an assignable entity within a type of equipment. The Equipment Entity Identification Format shall consist of a sequence of Family Code, Sub-Family Code, Features Code, Reference Code and Complemental Code data elements, resulting in a code that totals ten characters in the following format.</font><br/><font color="#29313b"><b>Components</b></font><br/><font color="#29313b"><b>Family Code</b></font><br/><font color="#29313b">A label to denote a functional category of manufactured telecommunications equipment. This data element shall consist of two characters, either of which may be alphabetic or numeric.  An example is D4, representing a D4 channel bank. </font><br/><font color="#29313b"><b>Sub-Family Code</b></font><br/><font color="#29313b">A label to denote a functional category of manufactured telecommunications equipment within a family. This data element shall consist of two characters, either of which may be alphabetic or numeric.  An example is CE, representing an E&M signaling channel unit.</font><br/><font color="#29313b"><b>Features Code</b></font><br/><font color="#29313b">A standardized code to denote a distinctive service-providing design capability available within a sub-family of manufactured telecommunication equipment when compared to other similar equipment of the same or other manufacturers. This data element shall consist of three characters, each of which may be alphabetic or numeric. Manufacturers’ examples are:</font><br/><ul>
<li><font color="#29313b">18B, where</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">1 signifies E&M signaling, 4 wire</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">8 signifies E with M lead applique, M lead resistor 510 ohm</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">B signifies with busy switch and level adjust attenuator</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">424, where</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">4 signifies DX signaling, 4 wire with post equalization</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">2 signifies adjustable balance capacitance and resistance</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">4 signifies impedance 150/600/1200 ohm</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">363, where</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">3 signifies DX signaling, 2 wire with gain transfer</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">6 signifies eliminate signaling condition, fixed balance capacitance</font></li></ul>
<ul>
<li><font color="#29313b">3 signifies receive enable circuit</font></li></ul>
<font color="#29313b"><b>Reference Code</b></font><br/><font color="#29313b">This is a label to denote the manufacturer’s information used to describe the design capability encoded in the equipment entity identification code. This data element shall consist of one character, which may be alphabetic or numeric.  Examples are:</font><br/><ul>
<li><font color="#29313b">7, signifying NNTM</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">8, signifying LEAR</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">P, signifying SD-7C026-02</font></li></ul>
<font color="#29313b"><b>Complemental Code</b></font><br/><font color="#29313b">This is a standardized code to provide uniqueness to an equipment entity identification code. This data element shall consist of two characters, either of which may be alphabetic or numeric.  (Examples of typical information are manufacturing vintage, version data, circuit wiring, or equipment data.) Examples are:</font><br/><ul>
<li><font color="#29313b">AA, for Northern Telecom, signifying NNTM QPP357B</font></li><li><font color="#29313b">AA, for LEAR, signifying LEAR D4DX-4010-11</font></li></ul>
<ul>
<li><font color="#29313b">AB, for Lucent Technologies, signifying J98726SD-2Composites</font></li></ul>
